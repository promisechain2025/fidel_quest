Provenance of these audio clips
===============================

Generated with espeak-ng (Amharic voice, via the text2wav WASM build),
peak-normalized and encoded to 48 kbps mono MP3. Synthesized placeholder
voice: clear and correctly keyed, but robotic. Replace any file with a
human recording of the same name and the apps pick it up automatically.

Note: espeak-ng is GPL-3.0 (the engine); these generated waveforms are
program output. If the project ever needs certainty for commercial
distribution, replace with recorded audio.

Do NOT source clips by scraping Google Translate TTS - that violates
Google's Terms of Service. Legitimate upgrade paths: record a native
speaker, or use a licensed TTS API (e.g. Google Cloud TTS am-ET) with
your own account.

---
HUMAN RECORDINGS (2026-07): a native speaker recorded 5 families as full
family-row chants (m4a). Split on silence, trimmed with a 60ms onset pad,
loudness-normalized (EBU R128, I=-16), and encoded to 96 kbps mono MP3.

All 33 letter families now use the human voice for orders 1-7 (231 clips),
plus 25 first words. Amharic collapses same-sound twins to the base voice:
  hha, kha <- ha    ae <- a    khe <- ke    se <- sse
Distinct Tigrinya-only recordings are preserved under letters/ti/
(ae, hha, kha, khe, qhe) for a future Tigrinya pack.

Re-recorded to fix content errors: she (ሸ) (the first take sounded wrong),
chhe (ጨ) (was the only family still on espeak).

Still on the espeak placeholder: the order-8 labialized bonus forms
(<id>-8), which only the Classic mode uses. Missing audio (falls back to
the chime): the words hager (ሀገር) and hamer (ሐመር) were added after the
25-word recording, so ha/hha have no first-word clip yet.
